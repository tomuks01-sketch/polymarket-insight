How the signal is built

Each coin's signal is a label, not a forecast. It is assembled only from data you can check:

  • Momentum — whether the 24h, 7d and 30d changes point the same way.
  • Volatility — how much the real 7-day price series has swung (coefficient of variation).
  • Turnover — 24h volume relative to market cap (how much is actually trading).

We deliberately do not publish a "chance to go up/down" number. Nobody can compute that honestly from these inputs, and a fabricated probability would be the opposite of useful. When the data is thin or mixed, the signal simply says so. Derivatives and on-chain metrics (open interest, funding, liquidations, flows) are marked not available until a real data source is connected — they are never invented.

Crypto Pulse is descriptive analytics of public market data. It does not predict prices, does not give financial or investment advice, and makes no profit claims. Data from CoinGecko's public API; figures move continuously. Think for yourself.

A free, public companion to The Calibration. Roadmap: watchlists · alerts · CSV/JSON export · historical signal log — all built on the same honest, falsifiable basis, never on invented probabilities.
